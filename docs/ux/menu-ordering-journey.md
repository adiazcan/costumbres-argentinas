# User Journey

## Persona 1: On-Site Guest
- Goal: Read the menu quickly and tell the waiter what to bring.
- Context: Standing or seated in the restaurant, often on a phone, attention split by conversation.
- Success metric: Finds item, size, and price in under a minute.

### Stage 1: Access
- Doing: Opens the site from a QR code or short link.
- Thinking: "I just need the menu, not the whole story."
- Feeling: Impatient, task-focused.
- Opportunity: Show menu-first navigation and an explicit on-site mode.

### Stage 2: Browse
- Doing: Scans categories and searches for a flavor.
- Thinking: "How quickly can I find the exact pizza or empanada?"
- Feeling: Neutral, but impatient if scanning is slow.
- Opportunity: Large category tabs, quick search, visible result summary.

### Stage 3: Decide
- Doing: Compares sizes and prices.
- Thinking: "Which size should I ask for?"
- Feeling: More confident once price structure is visible.
- Opportunity: Keep price labels grouped and easy to parse.

## Persona 2: Remote Caller
- Goal: Choose food and call immediately to place an order.
- Context: At home or work, possibly on desktop first and then phone.
- Success metric: Finds promos and phone number without hunting.

### Stage 1: Landing
- Doing: Opens the site from search, social, or a shared link.
- Thinking: "Where do I call? Are there combos?"
- Feeling: Focused, mildly impatient.
- Opportunity: Persistent phone CTAs in header, hero, contact, and mobile bar.

### Stage 2: Narrow Choice
- Doing: Checks promos and then searches menu details.
- Thinking: "Can I call with the exact combo and size ready?"
- Feeling: More confident if promos and menu are adjacent.
- Opportunity: Place promos before contact and switch guidance copy to phone-order mode.

### Stage 3: Call
- Doing: Taps or copies the phone number.
- Thinking: "I want zero ambiguity when I place the order."
- Feeling: Confident if phone number and steps are explicit.
- Opportunity: Add a simple three-step order panel.
